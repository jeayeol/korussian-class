Sentence Structure

In Corussian, the basic sentence structure follows the Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) order. For example:

Example: The cat (subject) eats (verb) the mouse (object).

Verb Conjugations

Corussian verbs are conjugated based on tense, aspect, and mood. Here are the basic conjugations for the verb "to be":

  • Present: am, is, are
  • Past: was, were
  • Future: will be

Noun Cases

Corussian nouns can appear in different cases depending on their role in the sentence. The primary cases are:

  • Nominative: Used for the subject of the sentence.
  • Accusative: Used for the direct object of the sentence.
  • Genitive: Used to show possession.

Adjectives

Adjectives in Corussian agree with the nouns they modify in case, number, and gender. For example:

Example: The red (adjective) apple (noun) - both are in the nominative case.
